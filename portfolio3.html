Game Development
The is Level 1 for my first game I'm currently developing. Though nameless atm, I've dubbed it "Red Ball Run" for the time being, as the player is literally a red ball rolling past / dodging turrets. Its a top-down shooter with a focus on timing, positioning, and bullet-hell style scenarios.
Red Ball
This is the Red Ball, with standard WASD movement and Mouse1 and 2 to Fire. Mouse1 is a semi-automatic straight shot. Mouse2 is violant torrent of bullets with the force to send the player shooting backwards, causeing as much grief for the player as for the enemy.
Opposition
Currently the game consists of two types of static turrents for the player to dodge. The Reds that fire a periodical, slow-moving bullet in a straight line ahead of them when the player approaches them. The Greens work in much the same way, though their shots move far quicker but with a longer wind-up. The layout of the level is also full of pitfalls, that should the player fall into, will teleport the player back to the beginning of the level.
Level 1 revolves around navigating through a maze of walls and turret patterns to reach the end, ideally without being killed much. The level also includes a handful of breakable walls and 'fake walls' to spice up the gameplay with a lil secrecy.
